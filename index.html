Why CAIS?

The problem with averages

A batter scoring 35 off 25 balls against a tailender in a dead middle over looks identical to 35 off 25 against a pace ace in the powerplay with wickets down. Conventional strike rate and average flatten all of that context. CAIS does not.

The metric is built on PSL ball-by-ball data. Every delivery is re-priced before it hits the tally.

How CAIS is calculated

The full formula, every multiplier, every weight.

Batting CAIS

Each ball a batter faces is worth its raw run value (0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6) multiplied by three context weights:

CAISbat = Σ(runs × phasebat × pressure) ÷ balls × 100 × formavg
FactorValuesWhy
phasebat PP: 1.2 · Mid: 1.0 · Death: 1.3 Scoring in the powerplay (bowlers attack) and death (rare, hard) is harder than in middle overs.
pressure 1.0 → 1.3 Rises with wickets lost so far × over number. Runs under collapse are worth more.
formavg 1.0 → 1.45 Rolling 3-match run window. Runs on a purple patch score a little extra.

Bowling CAIS

Each delivery scores the bowler positively on wickets and negatively on runs conceded, with wicket value scaled by five factors:

wicket_value = 30 × phase×role × batter_tier × form × pressure
run_cost = runs_conceded × phasebowl × 0.5
CAISbowl = Σ(wicket_value − run_cost) ÷ overs

Phase × Role wicket multiplier — this is the heart of why pace powerplay wickets are golden:

PhasePaceSpin
Powerplay (1–6)2.0×1.5×
Middle (7–15)1.2×1.5×
Death (16–20)1.8×1.2×
FactorValuesWhat it captures
batter_tier0.75 · 1.0 · 1.2 · 1.5 Batter's career avg-SR percentile. Dismissing elite batters (top 25%) scores 1.5×; tailenders 0.75×.
form1.0 → 1.45 Same rolling window as batting — in-form batter = more valuable wicket.
role inferencepace vs spin If >55% of balls bowled are in overs 7–15 → classified as spinner.
pressure1.0 → 1.3Rises with match state wickets × over.

Design choices

Why 30 as the wicket base? A typical T20 batting innings is ~25 balls; 30 calibrates a wicket's impact roughly to a quarter-innings of top-phase run-scoring.

Why 0.5× run cost? Raw run cost would dominate the score. The half weight keeps wickets central while still punishing expensive overs.

Why pace-powerplay wickets get 2.0×? They're rare (pacers average ~1 wicket per 4 powerplay overs), flip momentum, and expose new batters immediately — the biggest single swing in T20.
